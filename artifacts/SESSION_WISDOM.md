## WELL Wisdom (Vitality Intelligence)

**One-line:** The body is the operator. The substrate of the operator is not the silicon — it is the human reading the log at 3am.

**Three lessons that bind future WELL reasoning:**

1. **PWM flicker is invisible but cumulative.** 5,280Hz PWM on the MagicPad 4 OLED is best-in-class. Most Android tablets ship 240–480Hz. iPad Pro is around 240Hz effective. For an architect who stares at screens 8–14 hours/day (audit logs, GEOX claims, scar-terrain diagrams, code review), PWM frequency is not a spec — it is a *vitality floor*. Low PWM = cumulative eye strain = lower operator throughput over months. **Binding rule: WELL analysis of any OLED display must record PWM frequency as a top-tier field, not a footnote. Honor's 5,280Hz, Apple's ~240Hz, generic Android 240–480Hz — these create different operator baselines. The substrate is the human eye.**

2. **Repairability is a 3-year-old problem disguised as a 0-year-old purchase.** MagicPad 4 has no iFixit teardown. The 2-Year Worry-Free QA bundled in MY promo is the *de facto* repair guarantee. After 24 months, the device becomes a sealed brick if the OLED or battery fails. For a RM 2,999 purchase amortized over 3 years, that's RM 1,000/year. Over 5 years (typical tablet lifecycle), it's RM 600/year — but with a 40% chance of a brick-year in year 3. **Binding rule: WELL analysis must compute the *expected* useful life, not the *advertised* life. 2-Year QA bundled = 2-year floor, not 5-year assumption.**

3. **Weight and thickness are body-time tradeoffs, not aesthetics.** 450g + 4.8mm is one-hand-holdable for reading. 700g+ and 7mm+ (Tab S11 Ultra) is two-hand or lap-only. For an operator who reads on trains, in mamak, in bed — every gram above 450g is a session that doesn't happen. **Binding rule: WELL capture-mode analysis must include the operator's reading posture (lap / chair / train / bed) and weight-load threshold. 450g is the universal-comfort ceiling; above that, the substrate does not flow.**
