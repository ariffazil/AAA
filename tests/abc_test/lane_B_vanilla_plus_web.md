## PETRONAS 2024: The Real Numbers Behind the Rhetoric

A close reading of PETRONAS's Integrated Report 2024 and audited financial statements — verified against publicly filed documents at the Companies Commission of Malaysia — produces a more sober picture than the public narrative suggests.

### Verified Fiscal Contribution (FY2024)

The headline figure: PETRONAS contributed **RM72.4 billion** to the Government of Malaysia in 2024. This is not just dividend. The full breakdown, from the Integrated Report:

| Component | RM billion |
|---|---|
| Dividend to Government | 32.0 |
| Taxes (petroleum income tax, corporate income tax, state sales tax, export duties, statutory) | 26.8 |
| Cash payments to Federal and State Governments (for hydrocarbon value) | 13.1 |
| National Trust Fund (Kumpulan Wang Persaraan) | 0.5 |
| **Total** | **72.4** |

For comparison: 2024 federal revenue was approximately RM322 billion, so PETRONAS contributed roughly 22-23% of total federal revenue — still substantial, but the often-cited "30% of federal budget" figure reflects peak years around 2011-2014, not the current ratio.

The dividend of RM32 billion was declared on 14 March 2024 and paid in instalments through December. A subsequent dividend of the same amount (RM32 billion) was declared on 24 February 2025 for the financial year ending 31 December 2025. This is important: the 2025 dividend is already declared and largely committed, so the "dividend collapse" narrative for 2025 is overstated.

### The Actual Financial Position

From the audited statements:
- **Profit after tax:** RM55.1 billion (down from RM80.7 billion in 2023, a 32% drop — driven by lower average selling prices for crude oil and gas, partially offset by higher sales volumes)
- **Total assets:** RM766.7 billion (down 1% from RM773.3 billion in 2023)
- **Shareholders' equity:** RM451.2 billion (up from RM443.5 billion)
- **Cash flows from operations:** RM102.5 billion (down from RM114.2 billion in 2023)
- **Revenue:** RM305.1 billion (essentially flat vs RM305.8 billion in 2023)

This is a company with very strong liquidity. RM102 billion of operating cash flow is more than enough to cover capex, dividend, and debt obligations. The "near-collapse" framing has no basis in the audited numbers.

### The Gentari Question

The widely circulated claim that "Gentari burns RM1-1.5 billion annually" without generating profit is **not verified** in PETRONAS's public filings. Segment-level financials for Gentari are not separately disclosed in the consolidated accounts — only key subsidiary financials are required to be reported under Malaysian accounting standards.

What IS publicly disclosed: Gentari has secured **8 gigawatts of cumulative global renewable energy capacity** (installed and under construction) and was named **Energy Transition Company of the Year** at the 2024 Asian Energy Awards. It has installed over 1,000 EV charging points globally. PETRONAS describes Gentari as a "key driver in diversifying and future-proofing" the portfolio.

The honest framing: Gentari is in a heavy investment phase. Whether it is "burning cash" or "investing ahead of returns" depends on assumptions about the pace of energy transition and the valuation methodology for early-stage renewable assets. The "RM1-1.5 billion annual loss" figure cannot be confirmed from public sources and should be treated as unverified.

### The Divestment Pattern (Verified)

Two confirmed transactions:

1. **November 2025 — Eni JV (ADIPEC announcement):** $15 billion capex over 5 years, 19 assets (14 Indonesia + 5 Malaysia), 50:50 structure via UK-incorporated SEARAH SPV. Production target 500K boe/day. Operations begin July 2026. Source: PETRONAS and Eni joint statements, Reuters, Upstream Online.

2. **June 2026 — EnQuest PSC sale:** $833 million, 4 mature PSCs (Balingian, SK8, D35 in Sarawak + PM6/12 Peninsular). EnQuest is operator for 3 Sarawak assets, non-operator for PM6/12. Source: Reuters (10/6/2026), EnQuest PLC press release.

The EnQuest transaction is genuinely significant as the first major European operator entry at this scale. Whether it represents "strategic asset sale" or "portfolio rationalization with retained upside" depends on what the JV terms disclose — which has not been made public at this writing.

### Conclusion

PETRONAS is in a **mid-cycle compression, not a collapse**. The dividend is down 36% from peak (RM50B → RM32B), the PAT is down 32%, but liquidity remains abundant (RM102B operating cash flow) and the 2025 dividend is already declared at the same level. The Eni and EnQuest transactions are strategic moves in a multi-year portfolio rebalancing, not distress sales.

What remains genuinely uncertain and contested:
- Whether the Sarawak MA63 dispute will result in higher state royalties
- Whether Gentari's renewable investments will generate returns before the transition window closes
- Whether the federal-state petroleum arrangements will be restructured in the next 5-10 years
- Whether the Eni deal terms (still undisclosed) preserve Malaysian operational sovereignty

These are the real questions. They require Malaysian constitutional, fiscal, and political context to answer — not just financial document review.
