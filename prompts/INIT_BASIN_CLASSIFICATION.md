## 1. WHAT IS BASIN CLASSIFICATION?

A sedimentary basin is a region of prolonged subsidence where sediment accumulates
to significant thickness. Basin classification is the systematic categorization of
basins based on their **tectonic setting**, **crustal substrate**, and
**formation mechanism**. It is the fundamental framework for predicting
petroleum systems in frontier basins.

Before the plate tectonic revolution of the 1960s, basins were viewed as
"geosynclines" — a terminology that was confusing and misleading. The pioneering
work of **William R. Dickinson (1974, 1976)** established the actualistic
plate-tectonic framework that all modern classification schemes build upon.

## 2. THE THREE FIRST-ORDER CRITERIA (Dickinson 1974)

Basin classification rests on three independent criteria. Every basin can be
located in this 3-dimensional parameter space:

### 2.1 Type of nearest plate boundary

- **Divergent** — mid-ocean ridge, continental rift, pull-apart basin
- **Convergent** — trench, arc, back-arc, retro-arc foreland
- **Transform** — strike-slip, transpressional, transtensional

### 2.2 Proximity to plate boundary

- **Plate margin** — basin lies within the deforming boundary zone
- **Plate interior** — basin lies on stable lithosphere distant from any active boundary

### 2.3 Type of crustal substratum

- **Continental** — thick (30+ km) granitic/metamorphic basement
- **Oceanic** — thin (5–10 km) basaltic basement
- **Transitional** — extended continental crust, attenuated (15–25 km)
- **Anomalous** — obducted oceanic, ophiolitic, arc-derived, igneous/metamorphic
